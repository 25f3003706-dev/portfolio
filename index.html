About Me

My academic and technical journey began with a deep curiosity about how computation occurs at the physical level, which led me to explore VLSI design. As modern systems evolved toward intelligence and autonomy, I recognized that hardware alone is only part of the equation. To develop a complete understanding of contemporary computing systems, I expanded my focus to Artificial Intelligence with Machine Learning and Data Science, enabling me to work across the entire stack—from the transistor to the training loop.

I approach engineering as a systems thinker. Rather than treating software and hardware as isolated layers, I design solutions that account for hardware constraints, algorithmic efficiency, and data integrity simultaneously. I am driven by complex, multidisciplinary problems that demand a holistic and structured view of technology.
